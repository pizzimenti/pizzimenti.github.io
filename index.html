Project Objectives

  1. Add classes to style text
  2. Link email addresses
  3. Practice CSS selectors

This project was our introduction to styling text in large blocks that repeated in other places throughout our html document. We learned the differences between id and class and how to create a link to an email address.

About Me

Background

I was born in Portland. Though I spent my "uni years" travelling the globe and have only come back to reside here since 2014. Though I graduated from Portland State in 2007 with a degree in International Studies, I did my third year at the University of Buenos Aires. I also studied humanities and economics at Reed College for a year. In 2008 I was accepted to the Peace Corps and served in Burkina Faso for 27 months teaching computer literacy and building Ubuntu Linux labs. At the end of my service I received acceptance to the American University of Paris and the Sorbonne. After three years of working on Masters degree in International Relations and Sustainable Development, I set to writing my thesis on "Chinese Investment in Africa". In 2013 I travelled to Hangzhou to do research and discovered that what the Chinese are doing in Africa is happening too fast and too opaquely to be researched in traditional academic fashion. I realized that the changes shaping the world are not being prepared for in the ivory towers of academia but in research labs and in the office of startups around the world. I abandoned my thesis and returned home to become a part of shaping that change.

Languages

Though I am learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Swift right now, I am fluent in French and Spanish.

Hobbies

My hobbies are varied. I try dance contact-improv at least once a week. I occastionally DJ for ecstatic dance. I write fiction when code is not flowing for me. I rock climb and do yoga and meditate on a daily basis. Those three help set my day right! The rest is an exploration and the travel bug has not left my system entirely. I hope to return to it and interweave it with my career as a developer.
